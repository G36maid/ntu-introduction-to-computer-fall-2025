## Computers
A **computer** is an electronic device, operating under the control of instructions stored in its own memory.
*   **Process**: Accepts data (Input) -> Processing -> Produces information (Output).

### Types of Personal Computers
*   **Laptop**: Portable, screen and keyboard integrated.
*   **Tablet**: Slate style, touchscreen.
*   **Desktop**: Separate tower/monitor or All-in-One.
*   **Server**: Provides services to other computers or devices on a network (storage, resource control).

### Mobile and Game Devices
*   Smartphone
*   Digital camera
*   Portable and digital media player
*   E-book reader
*   Wearable device
*   Game device

## Data and Information
*   **Data (Input)**: A collection of unprocessed items (text, numbers, images, audio, video).
*   **Information (Output)**: Conveyed meaning to users (processed data).

### Input Devices
Hardware used to enter data and instructions.
*   **Text Input**: Keyboards (Standard, On-screen, Virtual).
*   **Pointing Devices**: Mouse, Touchpad (control a pointer on screen).
*   **Voice/Video**: Microphone, Webcam.
*   **Scanner**: Light-sensing device converting printed text/images into digital form.

### Output Devices
Hardware that conveys information to one or more people.
*   **Printer**: Produces text/graphics on physical media (Paper, 3D objects).
*   **Display**: Visually conveys text, graphics, and video.
*   **Speakers/Headphones**: Audio output.

### Memory and Storage
*   **Memory (RAM)**: Electronic components that store instructions waiting to be executed and data needed by those instructions. (Temporary/Volatile).
*   **Storage**: Keeps data, instructions, and information for future use. (Permanent/Non-volatile).
    *   **Storage Media**: The physical material (Hard Drive, SSD, USB, Disc).
    *   **Storage Device**: The hardware that reads/writes to the media.

#### Common Storage Types
1.  **Hard Disk Drive (HDD)**: Magnetic storage, spinning platters. High capacity, lower cost.
2.  **Solid-State Drive (SSD)**: Flash memory, no moving parts. Faster, durable, less heat.
3.  **External Hard Drive**: Portable HDD/SSD.
4.  **USB Flash Drive**: Portable flash memory plugging into USB port.
5.  **Memory Card**: Removable flash memory (e.g., SD cards for cameras).
6.  **Optical Disc**: CD/DVD, read/written by laser.
7.  **Cloud Storage**: Internet service providing remote storage (e.g., Google Drive, OneDrive).

## The Web
*   **Internet**: A worldwide collection of computer networks connecting millions of businesses, agencies, institutions, and individuals.
*   **World Wide Web (Web)**: A global library of information available to anyone connected to the Internet.
    *   **Webpage**: Electronic document.
    *   **Website**: Collection of related webpages.
    *   **Web Server**: Computer delivering requested webpages.

### Web Tools
*   **Browser**: Software to access and view webpages (e.g., Chrome, Safari).
*   **Search Engine**: Software to find information related to specific topics (e.g., Google).
*   **Online Social Network**: Website encouraging members to share interests, photos, stories (e.g., Facebook).

## Digital Safety and Security
*   **Malware**: Viruses, trojan horses, adware.
*   **Privacy**: Protection of personal identity and data.
*   **Health Concerns**: RSI, eye strain from prolonged use.
*   **Green Computing**: Reducing electricity and environmental waste (Recycling, Energy efficient hardware).

## Programs and Apps (Software)
Software tells the computer what tasks to perform.
1.  **System Software**:
    *   **Operating System (OS)**: Coordinates hardware operations (Windows, macOS, Android, iOS).
2.  **Application Software (Apps)**:
    *   Productivity tools (Word, PowerPoint), Browsers, Social Media apps.
    *   **Software Developer**: Person who writes the instructions/code.

---

## Communications and Networks
**Communications Device**: Hardware capable of transferring items to/from transmission media (e.g., Modems, Routers, Wireless Access Points).

### Network
A collection of computers and devices connected together (often wirelessly).
*   **Home Networks**: Connect to Internet, share devices, entertainment, smart home.
*   **Business Networks**: Facilitate communications, share hardware/data/software.

### Technologies
*   **Wired**: Cables.
*   **Wireless**: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Cellular radio.
